Homoeopathic Treatment for Major Diseases

×

Acidity

Acidity, or acid reflux, occurs when stomach acid flows back into the esophagus, causing heartburn and discomfort. It can be managed with dietary changes and medication.

×

Allergy

An allergy is an immune system response to a foreign substance, like pollen or pet dander. Symptoms range from mild irritation to severe reactions and can be managed with avoidance and medications.

×

Alopecia

Alopecia is an autoimmune disorder that causes hair loss on the scalp and other parts of the body. It can be temporary or permanent, and treatment options vary based on the type and severity.

×

Cervical Spondylitis

Cervical spondylitis is a degenerative condition affecting the cervical spine, causing neck pain and stiffness. It can lead to nerve compression, resulting in numbness and weakness in the arms. Treatment includes physical therapy and pain management.

×

Migraine

Migraine is a neurological condition characterized by recurrent headaches, often accompanied by nausea, vomiting, and sensitivity to light and sound. Treatment involves pain management, lifestyle modifications, and preventive medication for frequent sufferers.

×

Sciatica

Sciatica refers to pain that radiates along the path of the sciatic nerve, which runs from your lower back through your hips and buttocks and down each leg. It's usually caused by a herniated disk or bone spur on the spine compressing part of the nerve.

×

Acne

Acne is a common skin condition characterized by pimples, blackheads, and cysts. It often occurs during puberty and can be managed with proper skincare and medications.

×

Allergic Cough

An allergic cough is a persistent cough triggered by allergens such as dust, pollen, or pet dander. It is often accompanied by other allergy symptoms and can be relieved with antihistamines and avoiding triggers..

×

Arthrites

Arthritis is a group of conditions causing inflammation and pain in the joints. It can affect mobility and quality of life, with treatment focusing on pain relief and improving function.

×

Irritable Bowel Syndrome

Irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) is a common digestive disorder characterized by abdominal pain, bloating, and changes in bowel habits. Management involves dietary changes, stress management, and medication to relieve symptoms.

×

Piles

Piles, also known as hemorrhoids, are swollen veins in the lower rectum and anus. They can cause discomfort, itching, and bleeding during bowel movements. Treatment includes dietary changes, topical creams, and in severe cases, medical procedures.

×

Sinusitis

Sinusitis is inflammation or infection of the sinus cavities, causing symptoms such as facial pain, congestion, nasal discharge, and headache. It can be acute or chronic and is often treated with decongestants, nasal irrigation, antibiotics (if bacterial), and corticosteroids to reduce inflammation

×

Adenoids

Adenoids are lymphatic tissue in the back of the nasal cavity that help fight infections. Enlarged adenoids can cause breathing and ear problems, often requiring medical treatment or surgery.

×

Allergic Rhinits

Allergic rhinitis, commonly known as hay fever, is an allergic reaction to airborne substances like pollen. It causes symptoms such as sneezing, runny nose, and itchy eyes, and can be managed with medications and avoiding allergens.

×

Asthma

Asthma is a chronic respiratory condition characterized by inflammation and narrowing of the airways, leading to difficulty breathing, coughing, and wheezing. It can be managed with medication, avoiding triggers, and lifestyle changes.

×

Kidney Stone

Kidney stones are hard deposits of minerals and salts that form in the kidneys. They can cause severe pain when passing through the urinary tract. Treatment includes pain management, increased fluid intake, and sometimes medical procedures to remove larger stones.

×

Respiratory Infection

A respiratory infection refers to an infection affecting the respiratory tract, which includes the nose, throat, and lungs. It can be caused by viruses, bacteria, or other pathogens and can vary in severity from mild to severe.

×

Tonsillitis

Tonsillitis is inflammation of the tonsils, typically due to a viral or bacterial infection. Symptoms include sore throat, difficulty swallowing, fever, and swollen lymph nodes. Treatment may include rest, fluids, pain relievers, and antibiotics for bacterial infections.
